BLS / CPR

Skill I — Adult BLS / CPR
  1. Ensure scene safety — check surroundings for hazards
  2. Check responsiveness — tap shoulders, shout "Are you okay?"
  3. Shout for help; direct someone to call 911 and get AED
  4. Check for breathing and carotid pulse (no more than 10 seconds)
  5. If no pulse: begin CPR — 30 compressions : 2 breaths
  6. Compression depth: at least 5 cm; rate: 100–120 per minute
  7. Allow full chest recoil between compressions
  8. Head tilt–chin lift to open airway; give 2 rescue breaths (1 sec each, visible chest rise)
  9. Continue until AED arrives, trained rescuer takes over, or victim recovers
Skill II — Adult BLS with AED
  1. Follow all steps of Adult BLS / CPR (Skill I)
  2. Power on AED as soon as it arrives — do NOT stop CPR until AED is ready
  3. Attach pads: one below right collarbone, one below left armpit
  4. Plug in connector and let AED analyze rhythm — stand clear, no touching
  5. If shock advised: ensure no one is touching patient, press shock button
  6. Immediately resume CPR for 2 minutes after shock
  7. If no shock advised: continue CPR and follow AED prompts
Skill III — Infant BLS / CPR
  1. Check scene safety; check responsiveness (flick foot sole)
  2. Send someone to call 911; if alone, give 5 cycles of CPR first
  3. Check brachial or femoral pulse (no more than 10 seconds)
  4. If no pulse: place 2 fingers (middle and ring) just below the nipple line on the sternum
  5. Compress at least 4 cm deep; rate 100–120 per minute
  6. 30:2 ratio (1 rescuer) or 15:2 (2 rescuers)
  7. Neutral head position to open airway; give gentle puffs (watch for chest rise)
Skill IV — Severe Bleeding & Shock
  1. Don gloves (PPE) before touching blood or bodily fluids
  2. Apply dressings over the wound; press firmly with heel of hand
  3. Stack second hand on top — maintain constant, firm pressure
  4. Do NOT remove dressings — add more on top if soaking through
  5. If limb: apply tourniquet 5–8 cm above wound (not on a joint); note time
  6. Watch for shock signs: pale/cool/clammy skin, rapid weak pulse, confusion
  7. Have victim lie down, keep warm, call 911
Skill V — Choking (Adult / Child)
  1. Confirm severe airway block: unable to speak, cough, or breathe
  2. Ask "Are you choking? Can I help?" — if yes, say "I'm going to help you"
  3. Stand or kneel firmly behind the victim
  4. Make a fist — place thumb side just above navel, well below breastbone
  5. Grasp fist with other hand; give quick, firm upward thrusts
  6. Continue until object is expelled or victim becomes unresponsive
  7. If unresponsive: lower to floor, begin CPR; look in mouth before each breath
Skill VI — Open Fracture & Splinting
  1. Call 911; keep victim still — do NOT try to straighten the bone
  2. Cover any open wound / protruding bone with a clean dressing
  3. Immobilize using a splint extending beyond the joints above and below the fracture
  4. Pad the splint for comfort; secure with bandages — not too tight
  5. Check circulation, sensation, and movement below the injury before and after splinting
  6. Elevate the limb if possible to reduce swelling
  7. Apply ice wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling (not directly on skin)
Skill VII — Severe Burns
  1. Ensure safety; if person is on fire: Stop, Drop, and Roll
  2. Cover with a wet blanket to extinguish flames
  3. Call 911 for large or severe burns
  4. Cool the burn immediately with cold (not ice-cold) running water for at least 10 minutes
  5. Remove jewelry and clothing that is NOT stuck to the skin
  6. Cover with dry, non-stick, sterile dressings
  7. Cover victim with dry blanket; monitor for shock
Skill VIII — Heat-Related Emergency
  1. Move victim to a cool place; call 911 if heat stroke suspected
  2. Remove excess clothing
  3. Cool immediately: cool water spray, cool damp cloths on forehead, neck, armpits, groin
  4. Immerse in cool water up to neck if available (for heat stroke)
  5. If responsive and able to swallow: give fluids with sugar and electrolytes
  6. Stop cooling when victim behaves normally (to prevent hypothermia)
  7. If unresponsive and not breathing normally: begin CPR
Skill IX — Administer Epinephrine Pen
  1. Confirm signs of anaphylaxis: hives, swelling, difficulty breathing, low BP
  2. Call 911 immediately
  3. Remove epinephrine pen from case; remove safety cap
  4. Grip firmly in dominant hand
  5. Press tip firmly against outer mid-thigh (can be given through clothing)
  6. Push down until a click is heard; hold in place for 10 seconds
  7. Remove, massage the area, note time of injection; dispose in sharps container
Skill X — Administer Naloxone (NARCAN)
  1. Recognize signs of opioid overdose: unresponsive, slow/absent breathing, blue lips
  2. Call 911; ensure scene is safe
  3. Check for breathing — if not breathing normally, begin CPR
  4. Peel back foil packaging; hold device with thumb on bottom, two fingers on nozzle
  5. Tilt head back; support neck; insert nozzle into one nostril
  6. Press thumb firmly to release full dose
  7. If no response after 2–3 minutes, give second dose in other nostril; continue CPR as needed

ℹ️ About MedPath

What is MedPath?

MedPath is a free educational app that helps students and curious learners explore healthcare concepts — including anatomy, clinical reasoning, medical terminology, and pathophysiology.

Who is it for?

  • High school students exploring a future in healthcare
  • SHSM / HOSA students preparing for programs and competitions
  • Pre-med and future nursing students
  • Anyone curious about how the human body works

What can I learn?

  • Case Mode — clinical reasoning through patient scenarios
  • Anatomy Explorer — body systems, organs, and conditions
  • Flashcards — medical vocabulary and key concepts
  • Quiz Mode — scored practice with feedback
⚕️ Important Disclaimer

This app is for Educational purposes only. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. If you or someone you know has a health concern, please consult a qualified healthcare professional. Nothing in this app should be used to make real medical decisions.
